How an AI trading terminal works

Five AI agents read the same ticker from five different angles — chart structure, sentiment, fundamentals, risk, and macro — and return one probabilistic assessment in seconds. Here is exactly what happens between your click and the answer.

What an AI trading terminal actually does

A traditional charting platform shows you data and leaves the interpretation to you. An AI trading terminal closes that gap: it ingests live market data, runs it through analytical models, and returns an interpretation — key levels, setup quality, risk context — in plain English. You still make the decision. The terminal compresses the hours of analysis that decision used to require into seconds.

Tradolux does this with five specialized agents that run simultaneously on every ticker you analyze, using a live IEX data feed with sub-second updates.

The five agents, and what each one reads

  • Chart Intelligence — price structure, support and resistance, VWAP, moving averages, volume. Answers: what is the tape actually doing?
  • Sentiment — social volume, news flow, options skew. Answers: what is the crowd positioned for?
  • Fundamental — revenue growth, margins, valuation versus sector. Answers: does the business justify the price?
  • Risk Management — ATR, volatility regime, stop distance, position sizing. Answers: how much can this trade hurt you?
  • Macro — rates, sector rotation, index context. Answers: is the market tide with you or against you?

Each agent returns its own verdict — bullish, neutral, caution, headwind — with a one-line justification. You see where they agree and, just as importantly, where they conflict.

From five reads to one probabilistic assessment

The agents' outputs are combined into a single assessment with a confidence score. When conditions align, the terminal generates a Smart Signal from nine detectors — power inflow, VWAP reclaim, opening-range breakout, failed breakdown, RSI divergence, and others — each with a defined entry, target, and stop. A signal is a structured hypothesis with its risk pre-attached, not a prediction.

Every observation is logged, resolved, and scored over time, so you can see how each agent and detector has actually performed — not just how it claims to perform.

What it deliberately does not do

No AI system predicts the future. Tradolux does not promise win rates, does not auto-trade your account, and is not financial advice. It is an analysis layer: it makes the state of the market legible fast, states probabilities and risk explicitly, and leaves the decision with you. Trading involves substantial risk of loss.

How traders fit it into a real routine

Swing traders run their watchlist through the terminal after the close: five reads per ticker, conflicts flagged, levels marked — a full end-of-day review in minutes. End-of-day options sellers use the Risk and Macro agents to sanity-check volatility regime and stop distance before selling premium into the next session. Intraday, smart alerts track your levels so you don't have to watch the screen.
